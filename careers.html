What most companies expect
The baseline is a B.E./B.Tech/MCA/M.Tech or M.Sc (CS) degree, 60% or 6.0 CGPA throughout all semesters, and no active backlogs at the time of application (some companies allow historical backlogs). Most drives cover the 2024, 2025, and 2026 batches — check the specific company. Education gaps above 1–2 years are usually flagged during background verification.

Typical selection rounds

  • Round 1 — Online test (aptitude + 2–3 coding questions)
  • Round 2 — Technical interview: DSA, CS fundamentals
  • Round 3 — Technical interview 2: projects, system design basics
  • Round 4 — HR (CTC discussion, cultural fit)
  • Platforms used: Cocubes, HackerEarth, HackerRank, AMCAT

Salary benchmarks (2026 fresher)

  • TCS / Infosys / Wipro — ₹3.5–6 LPA
  • Mid-tier product companies — ₹8–16 LPA
  • FAANG / MAANG — ₹20–50 LPA
  • Funded startups — ₹6–20 LPA (varies a lot)
  • Govt / PSU — ₹6–15 LPA + benefits
Government Jobs & PSU Recruitment for CSE
Stable, well-paying government and public sector jobs open to Computer Science engineers.
GATE is the master key. A valid GATE (CS) score opens doors to NIC, DRDO, BARC, IOCL, ONGC, GAIL, BEL, and 30+ other PSUs. Your score remains valid for 3 years from the result date.

GATE 2027 at a glance
GATE for CS (paper code: CS) is 100 marks over 65 questions in 3 hours. General Aptitude accounts for 15%, the rest is core CS. MCQs carry 1/3 negative marking; MSQ and NAT questions do not. Notification is expected in July 2026, with registration closing around September. The exam itself is in February 2027.

High-weight topics

  • Very high Algorithms, DBMS, OS
  • Very high Data Structures, TOC
  • Medium CN, COA, Compiler Design
  • Medium Digital Logic, Programming
  • GA section Verbal + Numerical Aptitude

Key dates (expected)

  • Notification July 2026
  • Registration Aug–Sep 2026
  • Exam February 2027
  • Results March 2027
  • Score validity 3 years

Internship Opportunities for CSE Students
Internships build your resume and frequently convert to Pre-Placement Offers. Apply in your 2nd or 3rd year if possible — don't wait for final year.
The PPO route is the most reliable path to a high-paying job. Companies like Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Adobe heavily hire from their intern pool. An offer from a summer internship skips every screening round. Apply during August–October for the following summer.

Where to find more internships
  • Internshala — largest India internship portal by volume; filter by stipend and location
  • LinkedIn — filter by "Internship" + "India"; easy apply + connect with current interns for referrals
  • Unstop — competitions that double as internship funnels
  • Company career pages — always the best source; set email alerts for "intern" openings
  • ISRO / NIC / CDAC — check their official sites periodically; batches are small but the work is genuinely different
  • IIT/IISc Summer programs (SRFP) — research internships via SERB and JNCASR if you're interested in the academic side

What actually improves your intern chances

A strong GitHub (5+ projects with real READMEs), Codeforces 1400+ or LeetCode 300+, and a one-page resume with numbers — "reduced API response time by 40%" beats "worked on backend." Referrals matter more than most people think: one message to a senior who interned somewhere can skip the OA entirely. Apply in August–October for summer roles; waiting until November means most seats are gone.

Bhubaneswar Startups & Tech Companies Hiring CSE Freshers
Odisha's capital has a fast-growing startup and IT ecosystem. Great if you want to stay close to home, work directly with product teams, or skip metro-city costs for now.
Backed by the Odisha IT Policy 2025 and the Startup Odisha scheme, Bhubaneswar is home to IT parks like Infocity SEZ and Info Valley — where TCS, Infosys, Tech Mahindra, and Deloitte all have centres. Beyond that, a small but genuine product startup scene has emerged. The city ranks around #18 among Indian startup cities and the cost of living is meaningfully lower than Bengaluru or Hyderabad.
Fresher CTC here is typically ₹3–6 LPA — lower than metro product companies but with some real advantages: shorter commutes, faster responsibility, and direct client interaction even for freshers. A few years of hands-on work here can open doors for lateral moves to larger companies elsewhere.

Preparation Roadmap for CSE Placements
A rough semester-by-semester plan. Adjust based on where you are right now — starting in 3rd year is fine, starting in 4th year is harder but not impossible.

Year-by-year timeline

  • Year 1–2 Master C++ or Python; start competitive programming early
  • Year 2 (Jul–Dec) DSA seriously: arrays, trees, graphs, DP
  • Year 2 (Jan–Jun) Apply for summer internships
  • Year 3 (Jul–Sep) Build 2–3 real projects; aim for LeetCode 300+
  • Year 3 (Oct–Dec) Off-campus drives start — apply broadly
  • Year 4 Final year drives; GATE prep if that route interests you

CS subjects you must know

  • DSA Arrays, Trees, Graphs, DP, Heaps, Tries
  • OOPs Inheritance, Polymorphism, Design Patterns
  • DBMS SQL, Normalization, Indexing, Transactions
  • OS Scheduling, Memory Mgmt, Deadlock
  • CN TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP, OSI, Routing
  • System Design LLD + HLD (for product roles)
Off-campus placement — step by step
1
Build the foundation first (months 1–4)

Complete Striver's A2Z DSA Sheet (450+ problems). Solve 150+ LeetCode problems across difficulty levels — don't skip the mediums. Pick one language and know it well, either Java or C++. Keep your GitHub active with at least 3 visible projects that have real README files, not just code dumps.

2
Register everywhere — do it now, not later

TCS NextStep, Infosys InfyTQ, Wipro Careers, Cognizant, LinkedIn, Naukri, Internshala, Unstop. Set keyword alerts: "SDE fresher", "CSE 2026 batch", "software engineer trainee". Takes 2 hours total and you'll thank yourself later.

3
Don't ignore aptitude

Most people fail the OA not on the coding questions but on the aptitude and verbal sections. Most off-campus drives start with a 2–3 hour online assessment: aptitude, logical reasoning, and 2–3 coding questions. Prepare all three. PrepInsta and IndiaBIX have company-specific question sets.

4
August to December is peak season — be ready

About 80% of off-campus drives open in this window. Apply to at least 20–30 companies. Wide net, not just your dream companies. Track everything in a spreadsheet (company, applied date, status, next step). Join the Telegram channels that post daily alerts — drives close in 48 hours sometimes.

5
Mock interviews, not just solo practice

Pramp and Interviewing.io connect you with real partners. Revise CS fundamentals — OS, DBMS, CN, OOPs. Be ready to explain your projects completely: what you built, why, how it works, what you'd do differently. Interviewers probe exactly where your explanation gets vague.

6
GATE is a parallel track, not a backup

Start from Year 3 if this route interests you. A score above 650 opens NIC, DRDO, IOCL, ONGC, and BARC simultaneously. NPTEL lectures + PYQs is the standard approach that works.

7
Keep competing

SIH, TCS CodeVita, HackWithInfy, and one Codeforces/LeetCode contest per week. Even reaching SIH finals is worth a resume line. Contest ratings are strong differentiators when your degree and CGPA are identical to hundreds of other applicants.

Resume checklist

  • Length Strictly 1 page for freshers — no exceptions
  • Projects 2–3, each with tech stack and an impact number
  • CP links Codeforces/LeetCode profile with rating visible
  • GitHub Active repos with README files, not empty
  • Certifications AWS, GCP, NPTEL — only ones you completed

Common mistakes

  • Applying late — drives close in 2–5 days
  • Ignoring aptitude; it filters 60% of candidates before coding
  • Tutorial projects with zero modification
  • Skipping HR prep — culture fit questions do matter
  • Relying only on your college placement cell
