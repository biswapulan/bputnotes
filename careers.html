Bhubaneswar's IT scene is smaller than Bengaluru's or Hyderabad's, but it's growing fast — freshers get real responsibility sooner, and the commute is nothing. The list below spans IT services, fintech, healthtech, deep-tech and edtech, not just early-stage startups.

Backed by the Odisha IT Policy 2025 and the Startup Odisha scheme, the city hosts IT parks like Infocity SEZ and Info Valley, where TCS, Infosys, Tech Mahindra and Deloitte run centres. A genuine product-startup scene has taken root alongside them — Bhubaneswar ranks around #18 among Indian startup cities.
Fresher CTC here typically runs ₹2.5–8 LPA — lower than metro product companies, but with shorter commutes, faster ownership, and direct client contact even as a fresher.

What most companies expect
The baseline: a B.E./B.Tech/MCA/M.Tech or M.Sc (CS) degree, 60% or 6.0 CGPA throughout all semesters, and no active backlogs at application time (some companies allow historical backlogs). Most drives cover the 2024, 2025 and 2026 batches — check per company. Education gaps above 1–2 years are usually flagged during background verification.
  • Round 1Online test — aptitude + 2–3 coding questions
  • Round 2Technical interview — DSA, CS fundamentals
  • Round 3Technical interview 2 — projects, system design basics
  • Round 4HR — CTC discussion, cultural fit
  • Platforms usedCocubes, HackerEarth, HackerRank, AMCAT

GATE 2027 at a glance
GATE for CS (paper code: CS) is 100 marks over 65 questions in 3 hours. General Aptitude is 15% of the paper, the rest is core CS. MCQs carry 1/3 negative marking; MSQ and NAT questions don't. Notification is expected in July 2026, registration closes around September, and the exam runs in February 2027.

High-weight topics

  • Very high Algorithms, DBMS, OS
  • Very high Data Structures, TOC
  • Medium CN, COA, Compiler Design
  • Medium Digital Logic, Programming
  • GA section Verbal + Numerical Aptitude

Key dates (expected)

  • Notification July 2026
  • Registration Aug–Sep 2026
  • Exam February 2027
  • Results March 2027
  • Score validity 3 years

What actually improves your intern chances

A strong GitHub (5+ projects with real READMEs), Codeforces 1400+ or LeetCode 300+, and a one-page resume with numbers — "reduced API response time by 40%" beats "worked on backend." Referrals matter more than most people think: one message to a senior who interned somewhere can skip the OA entirely. Apply in August–October for summer roles; waiting until November means most seats are gone.

Bookmark four or five of these and check them daily — opportunities here close in 2–5 days, so passive browsing won't get you far.

A rough month-by-month plan. Start wherever you are — third year is fine, fourth year is tighter but not impossible.

Year-by-year timeline

  • Year 1–2 Master C++ or Python; start CP early
  • Year 2 (Jul–Dec) DSA seriously: arrays, trees, graphs, DP
  • Year 2 (Jan–Jun) Apply for summer internships
  • Year 3 (Jul–Sep) Build 2–3 real projects; LeetCode 300+
  • Year 3 (Oct–Dec) Off-campus drives start, apply broadly
  • Year 4 Final year drives; GATE prep if that route interests you

CS subjects you must know

  • DSA Arrays, Trees, Graphs, DP, Heaps, Tries
  • OOPs Inheritance, Polymorphism, Design Patterns
  • DBMS SQL, Normalization, Indexing, Transactions
  • OS Scheduling, Memory Mgmt, Deadlock
  • CN TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP, OSI, Routing
  • System Design LLD + HLD (for product roles)
Off-campus placement, step by step
01
Build the foundation first (months 1–4)

Complete Striver's A2Z DSA Sheet (450+ problems). Solve 150+ LeetCode problems across difficulty levels — don't skip the mediums. Pick one language and know it well, Java or C++. Keep your GitHub active with 3+ visible projects that have real README files.

02
Register everywhere, do it now

TCS NextStep, Infosys InfyTQ, Wipro Careers, Cognizant, LinkedIn, Naukri, Internshala, Unstop. Set keyword alerts: "SDE fresher," "CSE 2026 batch," "software engineer trainee." Takes 2 hours total.

03
Don't ignore aptitude

Most people fail the OA not on coding but on aptitude and verbal sections. Most drives start with a 2–3 hour assessment: aptitude, logical reasoning, and 2–3 coding questions. Prepare all three — PrepInsta and IndiaBIX have company-specific sets.

04
August to December is peak season

About 80% of off-campus drives open in this window. Apply to at least 20–30 companies, not just your dream companies. Track everything in a spreadsheet. Join Telegram channels that post daily alerts — drives close in 48 hours sometimes.

05
Mock interviews, not just solo practice

Pramp and Interviewing.io connect you with real partners. Revise OS, DBMS, CN, OOPs. Be ready to explain your projects fully — what you built, why, how it works, what you'd do differently.

06
GATE is a parallel track, not a backup

Start from Year 3 if this route interests you. A score above 650 opens NIC, DRDO, IOCL, ONGC and BARC simultaneously. NPTEL lectures + PYQs is the standard approach that works.

07
Keep competing

SIH, TCS CodeVita, HackWithInfy, and one Codeforces/LeetCode contest per week. Even reaching SIH finals is worth a resume line. Contest ratings are strong differentiators when your degree and CGPA look identical to hundreds of other applicants.

Resume checklist

  • Length Strictly 1 page for freshers
  • Projects 2–3, each with tech stack + impact number
  • CP links Codeforces/LeetCode profile with rating visible
  • GitHub Active repos with README files
  • Certifications AWS, GCP, NPTEL, only completed ones

Common mistakes

  • Applying late — drives close in 2–5 days
  • Ignoring aptitude — it filters 60% of candidates
  • Tutorial projects with zero modification
  • Skipping HR prep — culture fit does matter
  • Relying only on your college placement cell
